What I'm checking

  1. 16px (favicon). Does the keystone read, or does it disappear into a dark blob? If the bronze dot is invisible, the mark collapses to "a slightly fancy A."
  2. 32px (small tile). Does the strokes-vs-keystone composition still parse?
  3. 60px (iOS home screen). Does it differentiate from neighbors (Notes, News, Books)?
  4. The metaphor problem. At small sizes the "arch + chest + keystone" reading collapses to A-shape. That's OK if the A-shape is distinctive.
  5. Hypothesis to validate: the mark works at 32px+ and degrades acceptably to 16px. If it doesn't, we either simplify (drop the keystone at small sizes — favicon-only variant) or accept that the favicon is just "A on navy."
