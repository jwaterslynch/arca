Welcome to PPP Flow

PPP Flow — Prioritisation, Productivity & Procrastination — is a single dashboard for focusing your effort on the goals that actually matter, both work and personal.

Most days are consumed by reactive work: emails, meetings, logistics, other people's priorities. This day-to-day has to happen — but left unchecked, it devours all the time you needed for the things that move the needle.

PPP Flow gives you a system to protect time for high-value work, track whether you're actually spending that time, and build a weekly rhythm of accountability to yourself.

AI Coaching (Optional)

PPP Flow can use AI to help prioritise tasks, coach your life practices, and generate insights from your data.

When active, the AI sees: your goals, open tasks, life practices, and weekly commitments. It does not see: passwords, financial account numbers, or data from other apps.

Choose your AI provider

Cloud AI

Use a major foundation model via API. Fast and capable. Your data is sent to the provider's servers but is not used for training. Retained up to 30 days for safety monitoring.

DeepSeek's API sends data to servers in China where national security laws can compel data handover. For privacy, consider running DeepSeek locally via Ollama instead.

Local AI (Ollama) — 100% private

Run an AI model directly on your machine. Nothing leaves your computer. Requires 32GB+ RAM and Ollama installed.

The setup wizard can detect Ollama, download a starter model, and configure PPP Flow to use it locally.

Set up later? No problem. Press Next and continue manual onboarding.

How PPP Flow works: focus on one primary goal, track controllable inputs (not just results), keep a visible scoreboard, and review weekly so priorities stay honest.

Set Your Primary Goal

If you could only achieve one thing in the next 30–90 days that would make everything else easier or more meaningful — what would it be?

A good goal is specific (not vague), measurable (you'll know when it's done), and time-bound (has a deadline). Think of it as: "From X to Y by when."

Daily Life Practices

Not everything important is a work goal. These are recurring personal practices — the daily habits that keep you grounded. Frequency matters more than ambition. Set a minimum target you can actually hit every day.

Your First Tasks

Now break your goal into concrete tasks you can work on this week. Each maps to one of two types:

Strategic — tasks that directly advance your primary goal. These are your inputs: the work that predicts whether you'll hit the target. Protect time for these.

Maintenance — everything else. Emails, admin, meetings, logistics. The day-to-day. It needs doing, but it shouldn't crowd out strategic work. This is the Eisenhower principle: important work rarely feels urgent, and urgent work is rarely important.
